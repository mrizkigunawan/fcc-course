Ki Hadjar Dewantara

Here's a quick story of Ki Hadjar Dewantara's life:
  • Ki Hadjar Dewantara was born in Pakualaman, Java on 2 May 1889
  • Ki Hajar Dewantara is a national hero and the father of education, his birth on May 2 is celebrated in Indonesia as National Education Day.
  • Ki Hajar Dewantara is the founder of the Taman Siswa School, an educational organization that provides opportunities for indigenous people to obtain equal educational rights like the nobility and the Dutch.
  • Ki Hadjar Dewantara has worked as a journalist and writer in several newspapers. He is also very active in social and political organizations. He is active in the da'wah section to socialize and increase awareness of the Indonesian people on the importance of unity and integrity in the nation and state.
  • In 1913, he was exiled to the Netherlands by the Governor General Idenburg for his writing entitled "If I were a dutchman" because the article contained the Indonesian people being asked to insult the Dutch government who held an independence party on their colony while reaching into the public's wallet to fund the party.
  • there he joined the Indische Vereeniging organization which is a forum for Indonesian students. he started his dream to improve the quality of indigenous peoples by studying education. Until finally managed to get the Europeesche Act. The Europeesche Act is a high-value educational field and will later become the basis for starting educational institutions that it founded
  • Ki Hajar Dewantara returned to his homeland in September 1919. Ki Hajar Dewantara founded an educational institution called the National Onderwijs Instituut Tamansiswa.
  • After Indonesia's independence, in the first cabinet of the Republic of Indonesia, Ki Hadjar Dewantara was appointed the first Indonesian Minister of Education. In 1957 he received an honorary doctorate or honorary doctorate from Gadjah Mada University.
  • He was very helpful in pioneering public education and was declared the Father of Indonesian National Education and his birthday on May 2 is celebrated as National Education Day every year.
  • Ki Hajar Dewantara breathed his last in Yogyakarta on 26 April 1959. He was buried in Wijaya Brata Park.
